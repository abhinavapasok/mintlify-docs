## Overview

Sales invoices record what you have sold to a customer and how much they owe you. An invoice can be created from scratch or converted from a sales order or quotation, and it drives your receivables: recording payments against an invoice updates the customer's outstanding balance, and returns are capped against what was actually invoiced and paid.

## Before you start

- **Permission needed:** _Invoices → View_ to browse, _Add_ to create, _Edit_ to modify. Ask an administrator if an option is missing for you.
- **Settings to review:** document numbering (invoice number format and next number), default tax mode (inclusive or exclusive), and rounding behavior.

## Key concepts

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Tax mode | Whether item prices already include tax (_inclusive_) or tax is added on top (_exclusive_). The mode changes how line totals and the invoice total are calculated. |
| Discount | A reduction applied per line or on the invoice total, as a percentage or a fixed amount. |
| Additional fee | Extra charges (for example delivery) added to the invoice total, with their own tax treatment. |
| Rounding | An automatic adjustment so the payable total lands on a convenient amount. Shown as its own line in totals. |
| Outstanding amount | Invoice total minus payments recorded so far. |

## Common workflows

### Create an invoice

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the invoice list and select Create">
    Go to **Sales → Invoices** and select the create action.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the customer">
    Search by name or phone number. The customer's billing details and outstanding balance load automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add items">
    Search the item list and set quantity and price for each line. Line totals update as you type, following the invoice's tax mode.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply discounts, fees, and taxes">
    Add line or invoice-level discounts, additional fees, and confirm the tax treatment. The totals panel shows the running breakdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record a payment (optional) and save">
    You can record a full or partial payment at creation, or save the invoice unpaid. After saving, the invoice appears in the list with its payment status.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Record a payment against an existing invoice

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the invoice">
    Find it in **Sales → Invoices** and open it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a payment">
    Choose the payment action, then enter the amount, payment mode, and date. Partial payments are allowed; the outstanding amount updates immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Edit an invoice

Open the invoice and choose the edit action. Items, discounts, fees, and payments can be adjusted; totals are recalculated with the same rules as creation. Changes are recorded in the audit log.

### Cancel an invoice

<Note>
  Cancelling is not the same as deleting — a cancelled invoice stays in the list for traceability, and linked stock and balances are reversed.
</Note>

## Field reference

| Field | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| Invoice number | Generated from document numbering settings. |
| Invoice date | The date used for reports and due-date calculations. |
| Tax mode | Inclusive or exclusive — see Key concepts. Set per invoice; defaults from settings. |
| Discount | Percent or amount, per line or on the total. |
| Additional fee | Named extra charge with its own tax treatment. |
| Rounding | Automatic adjustment line on the total. |

## FAQs

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why did my line total change when I switched tax mode?">
    In inclusive mode the entered price already contains tax, so the pre-tax amount is derived from it. In exclusive mode tax is added on top of the entered price. Switching modes recalculates every line.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I refund more than the customer paid?">
    No. Refunds issued through returns are capped by the amount actually paid against the invoice.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who changed this invoice?">
    Administrators can see every change in the audit log, including who made it and when.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
